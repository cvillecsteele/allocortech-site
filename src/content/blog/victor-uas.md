VICTOR (the Versatile Intelligence-Controlled Tactical Operations Reconnaissance platform) is the US Army's Group 3 UAS. 12+ hours of endurance, 1,320-pound maximum takeoff weight, 400-pound payload capacity, and a 200+ nm radius. It operates in environments that demand −40°C to 50°C temperature range and EMI hardening for contested electromagnetic zones.

Allocortech components are integrated throughout the vehicle's power and avionics architecture.

The modular payload architecture accommodates electronic warfare systems and loitering munitions in addition to the primary ISR payload.

## Allocortech Systems on VICTOR

**Lohner Smart Batteries** provide standby power with DO-160 qualification and 1,500+ demonstrated cycle life, providing the availability and longevity a multi-year program requires.

**Mariner 400V Power Distribution Unit** (Clio-derived architecture, 285–325g, IP64) handles high-voltage distribution with the fault detection and management capability a 400V system demands.

**Taurus 400V Generator Control Unit** manages up to 32kW peak loads with ±80A regeneration, supporting hybrid power generation from the vehicle's engine through the same motor controller architecture used for propulsion.

**Taurus 400V Motor Controllers** operate across the full −40°C to 70°C temperature range with 20% thermal overhead reduction versus prior designs, translating to approximately 15% loiter time improvement in comparable UAS applications.

**Atlas Flight Controller** provides redundant autonomous navigation with Comet FTS integration and open SDK access for the mission-specific software modifications that operational programs inevitably require.
