2024 was Allocortech's best year by every measure that matters.

Revenue grew more than 50%. Product sales surged 256%. We launched two new commercial motor controller variants. And one of our longest-running customer programs, Navier's N30, crossed the line from prototype to commercial delivery.

## By the Numbers

- **50%+** revenue growth year-over-year
- **256%** surge in product unit sales
- **2** new COTS motor controllers launched (Taurus AEX and custom OTS hybrids)
- **1st** commercial electric hydrofoil delivery, powered by Dauphine

## Taurus AEX: A Better Way to Pack High-Power Density

The Taurus AEX extends our actuator-edition motor controller to higher voltage configurations (16S, 20S, 24S) while reducing thermal overhead by 20% in high-density installations compared to prior designs. For eVTOL booms where every millimeter and gram of thermal budget counts, that 20% matters.

## Lohner Smart Battery: Standby Power, Redesigned

The Lohner Smart Battery shipped to multiple programs in 2024, bringing high-availability standby power with real-time state estimation to platforms that previously relied on simple passive battery circuits. The standby-power architecture (automatic switchover without a software command) is the kind of safety-by-design feature that DO-160 qualification testing validates, and that operators appreciate when the primary bus has an issue at altitude.

## Navier N30: Commercial Delivery

In October 2024, Navier delivered the N30 Pioneer Edition to its first commercial customer at St. Francis Yacht Club in California. The first all-electric hydrofoiling boat built by a US manufacturer, with 100nm range, active foil control at 50Hz, and an autodocking system that Dauphine manages in real time.

## What's Next

Integrated motor/motor controller solutions are in development, expected to cut vehicle integration time by 40% for the motor + controller pairing that currently requires separate procurement and mechanical coordination. Hornet ADAHRS deployments are showing 300g weight savings per aircraft and a measured 12% range improvement on eVTOL platforms where the weight reduction matters.

2025 is going to be busier.
