Northrop Grumman's decision to invest $200M+ and establish advanced electronics manufacturing operations at the Nature's Crossing Technology Center in Waynesboro, Virginia is a landmark moment for the region and a validation of the aerospace and defense ecosystem that has been quietly building here for years.

Waynesboro was selected over 50+ competing sites. That's not a coincidence. It reflects the concentration of aerospace talent, infrastructure, and technical capability that makes this valley a legitimate destination for prime contractors.

Allocortech was founded in Waynesboro five years before this announcement. We've been building safety-critical avionics hardware here since 2018: motor controllers, flight termination systems, and distributed I/O hubs, delivered to UAS and eVTOL programs across the country and internationally.

## The Regional Cluster Is Real

Dynamic Aviation in Bridgewater. The Shenandoah Valley Partnership. And now Northrop Grumman at scale. The cluster that aerospace economic development professionals talk about is materializing.

For prime contractors and Tier 1 integrators evaluating their supply chains, the Shenandoah Valley now offers what few regions can: proximity to the major defense corridors of Northern Virginia and Washington DC, access to strong engineering talent pipelines, and cost structures that make domestic production economically viable.
